Lesson 1: Basic introduction to Spanish

Spanish is one of the most widely spoken languages, with over 500 million native speakers! It comes from Latin and is part of the Romance language family, along with French, Italian, and Portuguese. Spanish is phonetic, meaning words are pronounced as they’re written, but watch out—small pronunciation mistakes can lead to hilarious misunderstandings! In this lesson, you’ll learn what makes Spanish unique, from its alphabet to its verb conjugations. Ready to dive in? ¡Vamos!

Lesson 2: Conjugations

Spanish verb conjugation changes the verb form to match the subject and tense. Regular verbs fall into three categories: -ar, -er, and -ir, each with specific endings in the present indicative. Person (who) and number (singular/plural) influence verb endings. "Vosotros" is mainly used in Spain, while "ustedes" is common in Latin America. Mastering present tense conjugations is essential for everyday communication.

Lesson 16: Imperfect past vs Simple past

Spanish has two main past tenses: preterite (for completed actions) and imperfect (for ongoing or habitual actions). The preterite is used for events with a clear beginning and end, often with time markers like ayer or anoche. The imperfect describes background details or repeated actions, using phrases like cuando era niño or todos los días. Choosing between them depends on context and how the action is viewed. Understanding both helps convey past experiences accurately in Spanish.
